A vast mahogany desk sits dead center in the room. A table lamp rests on top, sitting next to a dull black typewriter and another cardboard box.

On the far wall is a window, shrowded by dust caked curtains that could either be a deep maroon or dark brown. Through the glass you can see the front yard. There's your car, sitting empty and alone.

On another wall rests hundreds of books, some leather bound and others wrapped in parchment. None of them you've heard of, and it's also likely none have been added to the collection in the last century.

At least four different clocks perch in various places about the room. Every single one is stopped at the exact same time, 10:20.

There are a few papers strewn across the floor which must've been knocked from the desk.

From here, you can only return to the upstairs hallway.
